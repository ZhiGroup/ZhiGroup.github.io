**Computational genomics and pangenomics**

We develop scalable methods for population-scale genetic data, including IBD detection, haplotype sharing, genotype imputation, relatedness inference, local ancestry, and pangenomic representations. These methods are designed for large biobanks and diverse cohorts where hidden genetic structure can become a foundation for discovery.

**Clinical EHR deep learning**

We build representation learning and foundation models for longitudinal electronic health records. This includes Med-BERT, CovRNN, and related models for disease risk prediction, clinical trajectory modeling, and transfer learning across heterogeneous healthcare datasets.

**AI-powered imaging genetics and GWAS**

We use deep learning to extract image-derived phenotypes from biomedical imaging and connect those phenotypes to genetic variation through GWAS. This program targets disease-relevant signals that are difficult to capture with conventional labels, including neuroimaging endophenotypes and retinal phenotypes.
